Section 2 --- GotoMarket & City Launch Plan

Expanding the business city by city requires a structured go-to-market
strategy combined with a detailed launch plan for each new location.
Successful geographic expansion is grounded in clear metrics, phased
milestones, and effective knowledge transfer to local teams. In this
section, we outline a comprehensive plan for entering new cities, from
initial market research through post-launch optimization, ensuring each
launch is executed efficiently and sets the stage for sustainable
growth.

2.1 Market Research & City Selection

Before committing to any city launch, we will conduct in-depth market
research to select the right city and prepare a data-driven entry
strategy. Key factors to research include:

Local Market Demographics: Understand the target city's population
characteristics and customer segments to ensure our product resonates
with local needs.

Competitive Landscape: Identify existing competitors or alternatives in
the city and benchmark their offerings and market share. This analysis
will highlight gaps we can fill and help position our service
effectively.

Regulatory and Legal Requirements: Check for any city-specific licenses,
permits, or regulations that could affect operations. For example, some
cities may require special permits for our services that we don't need
in current markets, and we must secure these in advance to launch
legally and smoothly.

Operational Fit: Assess whether our current business model and processes
will work in the new city, or if we need adaptations. This is especially
crucial for an expansion into a very different market or culture.

In addition to the above, financial viability will be evaluated for each
potential city. We will forecast the launch and operating costs versus
projected revenues to ensure the expansion makes economic sense. Only
cities that show a healthy opportunity (sufficient target customers and
manageable competitive pressure) will be chosen for launch.

Upfront preparation is also critical. Prior to expansion, we will
establish a clear set of Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and document
standard processes as the foundation for all city launches. These KPIs
(e.g. customer acquisition rate, monthly active users, local revenue,
etc.) define what success looks like in a new city. They enable us to
set measurable milestones for the launch (such as acquiring a certain
number of users or providers within the first 3 months). By having a
