Contract formation: ToS acceptance on signup; changes notified;
continued use indicates acceptance.\
• Eligibility: 18+, US resident; no minors in content; no use where
prohibited by law.\
• Fees & payment: buyer service fee, provider fee, taxes, deposits; all
itemized; off‑platform payment encouragement prohibited.\
• Escrow specifics: timing of charges/holds; release triggers;
cancellation and refund math summary; deposits distinct from GMV.\
• Licenses: provider retains IP unless a specific SOW says otherwise;
platform receives a limited license to host/transform content for
features.\
• AUP core rules: no explicit sexual content in public; no harassment,
hate, doxxing, or illegal activity; respect privacy; no dangerous
requests.\
• Safety features: Safe‑Mode defaults; reporting tools; IDV; background
checks; reputation score logic (transparency without revealing raw
signals).\
• DMCA/notice‑and‑takedown: agent info; notice requirements;
counter‑notice path and timeline.\
• Arbitration/class action: optional clause with opt‑out window;
small‑claims carve‑out (to be confirmed with counsel).\
• Disclaimers: we do not employ providers; we facilitate bookings; we
are not liable for off‑platform conduct; limit damages as permitted by
law.\
• Termination: grounds for account limits/closure; appeal process; data
export on closure where feasible.

Procedures & Operational Playbooks:\
• ToS update cadence with versioning and redline summaries; banner
notices for material changes.\
• Privacy data maps kept current; DSR (data subject request) intake with
standard verification steps.\
• AUP enforcement queue: content moderator workflow integrated with
Safe‑Mode and strike system.

Enforcement & Remedies:\
• Graduated responses: content removal, feature restrictions, temporary
holds, permanent disable.\
• Recurring abuse: strike thresholds; Trusted Pro badge revocation;
promotions ineligibility.\
• DMCA compliance: remove on valid notice; restore on counter‑notice
unless suit filed; repeat infringer policy.

Data, Privacy & Retention:\
• What we collect: account, verification, booking, payment tokens,
device, and limited log data; no unnecessary sensitive categories.\
• How we use data: to provide services, safety, payments, and analytics;
no sale of PII; limited sharing with processors and vendors.\
• Retention: contracts/releases 7 years; booking and finance records per
finance policy; deletion on schedule except legal holds.

Templates & Required Notices (internal/external):\
• ToS last‑updated banner; privacy data inventory; DMCA agent contact;
AUP visual examples sheet; strike system explanation; arbitration
opt‑out instructions.\
• Email/SMS templates: material change notices; enforcement notices;
DMCA responses.

Compliance Metrics & Audits:\
• Policy comprehension (help‑center views, time on page); enforcement
accuracy (appeal upholds); DMCA SLA; DSR response time; privacy access
ledger review rate.

Risks & Mitigations:\
• Ambiguous terms → publish examples and FAQs; consult counsel on
arbitration options and state carve‑outs.\
• Over‑reach in licenses → limit to what is necessary to operate the
service; avoid assignment by default.

Acceptance Checklist (ready to externalize):\
□ Counsel review complete; public pages staged; AUP queue live; DMCA
agent set; versioning redlines prepared; strike policy connected to
reputation signals.
