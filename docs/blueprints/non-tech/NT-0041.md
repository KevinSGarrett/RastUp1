We will also encourage an environment of continuous knowledge sharing
among city teams. As the Times of India notes, effective expansion
relies on teams being "independent as well as centrally aligned and
sharing best practices". To facilitate this, we'll hold regular
cross-city meetings or calls (for instance, a bi-weekly "city leads
sync") where city managers exchange learnings, challenges, and
successes. We will maintain an internal knowledge repository (wiki or
playbook documents) that gets updated with insights from each launch --
this may include refining checklists, marketing tactics that worked, or
pitfalls to avoid. These updates will continuously enhance our city
launch playbook. In fact, each new city provides an opportunity to
improve the model: for example, after launching in our second city, we
might refine our onboarding process or marketing strategy based on what
we learned, creating an even stronger template for the third city. Over
time, this accumulation of experience becomes a powerful asset, making
subsequent launches more predictable and efficient.

Another post-launch strategy is to foster some exchange of talent for
training and culture. We can have experienced team members from a
successful city spend time in a newer city to mentor the local team or
help instill best practices, and vice versa. This cross-pollination
ensures that our company's culture and standards are consistently
spread, and it gives rising local stars a chance to learn in different
environments.

Finally, our expansion plan will take a sustainable growth approach. We
will only scale up to the next cities when ready, rather than launching
too many cities all at once. It's important to avoid the trap of growing
too fast, too soon, which can strain resources and lead to quality
control issues. Each city launch will be evaluated against its KPIs to
ensure it's stable (operationally and financially) before we green-light
the next launch. This paced expansion prevents high cash burn and
employee burnout that can come from aggressive, uncontrolled expansion.
By making sure we have the necessary resources (funding, people,
technology) in place for each new market, we protect the quality of our
service and brand reputation as we grow.

In summary, the post-launch phase focuses on consolidating success,
maintaining standards, and preparing for the next expansion. We will
continuously monitor each city's performance and support the teams to
ensure ongoing success. Lessons learned will feed back into our
go-to-market strategy, allowing us to refine our approach with every new
city. This feedback loop and careful scaling strategy will enable us to
confidently expand our footprint city by city, while delivering a
consistent, high-quality experience in every market we serve.
