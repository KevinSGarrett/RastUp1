2.2 Launch Team & Local Operations Setup

To execute the city launch plan effectively, we will deploy a dedicated
city launch team and set up local operations in advance. Rather than
expecting each new city to figure things out independently, a specialist
launch team from the company will temporarily relocate to spearhead the
launch on the ground. This team may include experienced members from our
existing operations along with specialists (e.g. marketing, community
managers) who focus on new launches. Crucially, the team will include a
recruiter or HR lead who arrives about six weeks early in the target
city to start lining up local hires before the official launch period.
Hiring a local city manager and key operational staff in advance ensures
that when we launch, we have people who know the area and can eventually
take over daily operations.

During the initial weeks in a new city, the launch team will handle
tasks like securing any physical requirements (for example, a small
local office or storage facility if needed) and ensuring all logistics
and regulatory prerequisites are in place. This includes confirming
local permits or licenses have been obtained (as identified in the
research phase) and setting up any local partnerships or vendor
relationships needed for operations (e.g. local supply vendors,
last-mile logistics, etc.). Compliance with city regulations and laws
will be a priority before launch day, so that we enter the market on the
right side of local requirements.

We recognize that hiring the right local talent is pivotal to success in
a new market. The local team will ultimately run the city's operations
after the launch team leaves, so we will take care to recruit
high-quality, proactive individuals who can operate with some autonomy.
Especially if the new city is far from headquarters, we need employees
who are self-starters and don't require constant hand-holding. We will
not rush the hiring process or settle for sub-par candidates simply to
fill positions quickly; bringing on the right people (even if it takes a
bit more time) will pay off in smoother operations and better growth
post-launch. The recruiter on the launch team will focus on assembling
this local team early, so they can train alongside the launch experts
during the rollout period.

In summary, our go-to-market plan calls for a skilled launch taskforce
to establish each new city, build out local operations, and hand over a
well-functioning business to a carefully hired local team. By
front-loading the operational setup and knowledge transfer, we set each
city up with the tools, people, and processes it needs to thrive from
day one.

--- each new city requires a localized go-to-market strategy tailored to
its audience and culture. Our marketing plan for city launches will
combine overarching brand messaging with hyper-local tactics to quickly
build awareness and user adoption in the new market.
