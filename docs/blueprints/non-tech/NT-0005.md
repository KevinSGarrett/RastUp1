2\) Global Search UX (CategoryFirst)

Step 1 (Required): User selects a Role to search: Models / Photographers
/ Videographers / Creators.

Step 2: Search bar accepts keywords; Advanced Filters change per role.

Result Card = Service Profile (rolescoped): name, city, role label, role
rating, starting rate, 2--3 rolespecific highlights, verification
badges, followers/engagement (if connected), and Instant Book badge (if
enabled).

Clickthrough: Goes to the role page (e.g., /u/jane/model) with sticky
tabs to view Jane's other roles.

3\) Global Filters (apply to every role)

Location & distance radius

Available on (date/time picker) → "Only show profiles available on this
date"

Budget / starting rate (range)

Rating (min stars) & \# of reviews (min)

Verification (ID / Backgroundchecked / Socialverified)

Response time (e.g., "usually replies within 2h")

Completion / cancellation rate (reliability)

Instant Book toggle (see §7)

Languages; Travel willing (local only / regional / national)

Last active (e.g., last 7 days)

These are the "what buyers care about" filters---availability, price,
quality, trust, reliability, and speed.

4\) RoleSpecific Filters (what truly matters)

4.1 Models

Look & presence (high impact):

Height, measurements (bust/chest, waist, hips; cup optional),
dress/clothing, shoe

Body type, tattoos, piercings, hair (color/length), eye color, skin
tone, facial hair (if applicable)

Age (18+ only platform), ethnicity (selfdescribed)

Categories/levels (fashion, beauty, fitness, swim, lingerie, artistic
nude if allowed---see §9)

Commercial/operational:

Rate (hour/day), travel radius & travel fee, chaperone friendly, release
preference (e.g., standard model release)

Reliability signals: noshow rate, ontime rate, average reply time

Social signals (if connected): platform(s), followers range, engagement
rate, audience top geos, growth rate 30d (see §5)

Competitors already filter basics like hair/height/measurements; we add
reliability, availability, and audience quality---which they lack.

4.2 Photographers

Focus on result buyers see, not gear:

Specialties (portrait, fashion/editorial, beauty, commercial, product,
events)

Style descriptors (natural light, moody, highkey, cinematic, colortrue,
retouchedlook)

Deliverables (e.g., "# edited images", RAW available?, lookbook,
campaign, ecomm sets)

Turnaround (e.g., 48h, 1 week)

Studio access (yes/no), preferred shoot types (instudio, outdoor),
chaperone welcome

Rate (hour/day/packages)

Reliability (completion rate, ontime rate), rating

Social signals (if connected): followers, engagement, recent growth

We remove gear/software filters; nonphotographer buyers don't care. We
foreground style, deliverables, turnaround, studio access, price,
reliability.

4.3 Videographers

Content types (shortform reels, promo, music video, event, interview,
product)

Deliverables (duration formats, aspect---vertical/horizontal), \# of
revisions, sound capture (yes/no), captions (yes/no)

Turnaround, rate, travel

Style (snappy/reels, cinematic, docu, productclean)

Reliability, rating

Social signals (followers, avg views---if connected)

4.4 Content Creators / Influencers

Platforms (IG/TikTok/YouTube/...); posting frequency

Followers range, engagement rate, avg video views, story views (if
available)

Audience demographics (top countries/cities, age/gender splits) & brand
safety tag

Niches (beauty, fitness, cosplay, gaming, fashion, etc.)

Deliverables & rates (grid post, story set, reel/short, live, bundle),
lead time

Growth rate 30/90d, prior brand collabs

Reliability (ontime posting rate), rating

Verified social metrics & audience demographics are available via
firstparty APIs when the creator connects their accounts (details §5).
